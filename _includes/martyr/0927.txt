AT AEgea, during the persecution of Diocletian, 
the birthday of the holy martyrs Cosmas and 
Damian, brothers. After miraculously overcoming 
many torments from bonds, imprisonment, fire, crucifixion, stoning, arrows, and from being cast into the 
sea, they received capital punishment. With them 
are said to have also suffered three of their brothers, 
Anthimus, Leontius, and Euprepius.&mdash;At Rome, St. 
Epicharis, wife of a senator, who, in the same persecution, was scourged with leaded whips, and struck 
with the sword.&mdash;At Todi, the holy martyrs Fidentius and Terence, under the same Diocletian.&mdash;At 
Cordova, the holy martyrs Adulphus and John, 
brothers, who won the martyr's crown in the Arabian persecution.&mdash;At Sion, in Switzerland, St. 
Florentinus, martyr, who was put to the sword with 
blessed Hilary, after his tongue had been cut out. 
&mdash;At Byblos, in Phoenicia, St. Mark, bishop, who is 
also called John by blessed Luke.&mdash;At Milan, the 
holy bishop Caius, a disciple of the blessed apostle 
Barnabas, who passed calmly to rest after suffering 
severely in the persecution of Nero.&mdash;At Ravenna, 
St. Aderitus, bishop and confessor.&mdash;At Paris, St. 
Vincent de Paul, priest, and founder of the Congregation of the Mission and of the Daughters of 
Charity, an apostolic man and a father to the poor. 
His feast is celebrated on the 19th of July.&mdash;In the 
same city, St. Eleazar, count.&mdash;In Hainaut, St. Hiltrude, virgin.
